üåä What is an ECA?

Emission Control Areas (ECAs) are sea areas where stricter controls are in place to minimize airborne emissions from ships. These include Sulphur Emission Control Areas (SECAs) and Nitrogen Emission Control Areas (NECAs).

‚öñÔ∏è IMO 2020 Compliance

Under IMO 2020, ships must use fuel with max 0.5% sulphur globally. In ECAs, the limit is stricter at 0.1%. Non-compliance fines range from $15,000-$50,000+.

üí∞ Fuel Cost Impact

Low-sulphur fuel costs 30-50% more than standard marine fuel. Knowing your ECA exposure helps optimize routes and reduce fuel costs while maintaining compliance.
